Important Notes

  • RC522 is 3.3V only — do NOT connect to 5V or you will damage it. The PN532 replacement is also 3.3V logic.
  • The SSD1306 OLED is a stand-in for the eventual color IPS LCD (ST7789). The I2C OLED is easier to prototype with since it's only 2 data wires. The ST7789 will use SPI in the final design.
  • GPIO35, 36, 37 are not available on ESP32-S3 modules with octal SPI (N8R2, N16R8). If your module is N4 or N4R2, they're usable.
  • The on-board RGB LED might be on GPIO48 (v1.0 boards) or GPIO38 (v1.1 boards). Check which one works in firmware.
  • HX711 clock (SCK) can be shared across multiple HX711s in the final multi-slot design. For this single-slot prototype, direct wiring is fine.
  • Add 4.7kΩ pull-up resistors on SDA and SCL lines if I2C communication is unreliable. Many breakout boards include these already.
  • Load cell wiring: Red→E+, Black→E-, White→A+, Green→A- (verify with your specific load cell datasheet — colors vary).

Breadboard Layout Tips

  • Place the ESP32-S3 straddling the center channel of the breadboard
  • Use the top power rails for 3.3V and GND
  • RC522 can sit on a separate mini breadboard or use dupont wires from its header
  • HX711 module sits off-board near the load cell — use dupont wires to connect the digital side
  • The OLED module plugs directly into the breadboard or connects via Grove cable to the XIAO expansion board

GPIO Budget (single slot prototype)

  • Used: GPIO5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 48 = 10 pins
  • Free: GPIO0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 14, 15, 16, 17, 18, 19, 20, 21, 38, 39, 40, 41, 42, 45, 46, 47 = 21 pins
  • Reserved (octal SPI): GPIO35, 36, 37
  • At 3 pins per additional slot, that's room for 7 more slots = 8 total per ESP32-S3
